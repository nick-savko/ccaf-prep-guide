# CCAF Exam Prep: A Practice-First Plan

> ⚠️ **Unofficial guide.** Not created, reviewed, or endorsed by Anthropic. Links point to a mix of official Anthropic resources and third-party practice tests — when in doubt, defer to Anthropic's official materials.

## Who This Is For

A practice-first prep plan for the **Claude Certified Architect – Foundations (CCAF)** exam. It assumes you already have some hands-on experience with Claude and just need a tight, exam-focused path — not a full re-learn of the product.

**Core principle:** general courses, docs, and books are good for learning the product — they are not an efficient way to pass a specific exam. The fast path is repeated, focused practice testing: test → find the gaps → close the gaps → retest.

> **A personal note:** I used this exact approach to prepare for the CCAF exam and got there in about two weeks, roughly 16 hours of total study time. It's not necessarily the *ideal* path — just what worked for me. I originally put this method together while preparing for other cloud certifications (AWS, Azure) in the past, and adapted it here for CCAF. If you have suggestions or know of better materials, feel free to reach out — happy to improve this.

## Phase 1: Baseline
*Goal: find your starting point, learn the question style.*

- [ ] **Step 1.** Take the official Anthropic practice exam on Skilljar without any preparation. 

The point of doing it cold is to find out your actual baseline score and get a feel for the kind of questions the real exam asks. The platform shows you an explanation for the correct answer after each question, but at this stage you can skip reading them — you're not trying to learn yet, just to see where you stand.

---

## Phase 2: Orientation
*Goal: build a working map of what the exam covers, before testing yourself further.*

- [ ] **Step 2.** Read through the [exam domains guide](https://claudecertifications.com/claude-certified-architect/domains). Go through it thoughtfully rather than skimming, and whenever you hit something unfamiliar, stop and google it instead of pushing past it.
- [ ] **Step 3.** Read through the [exam scenarios](https://claudecertifications.com/claude-certified-architect/scenarios#scenario-1) the same way — thoughtfully, googling anything that doesn't make sense yet.

---

## Phase 3: First Full Practice and Deep Review
*Goal: surface real gaps under low-pressure conditions.*

- [ ] **Step 4.** Take the [Cyberskill mock exam](https://claude-certified-architect-mock-exam-cyberskill.vercel.app/#top) in untimed practice mode. You can split it across a couple of sittings, but try to stay within a few hours total so you don't lose your progress. While you're taking it, flag every question you're not fully confident about, even the ones you technically get right. The most important part of this step happens after the test: go through every question, identify everything you got wrong or were unsure of, read the explanations, and write your own notes on it.
- [ ] **Step 5.** Go back to the [official exam guide PDF](https://everpath-course-content.s3-accelerate.amazonaws.com/instructor%2F8lsy243ftffjjy1cx9lm3o2bw%2Fpublic%2F1773274827%2FClaude+Certified+Architect+%E2%80%93+Foundations+Certification+Exam+Guide.pdf) and focus specifically on the domains and topics that gave you trouble. 

> Skip the Sample Questions section for now — that's the next step, kept separate on purpose.

---

## Phase 4: Sample Questions
*Goal: self-test on official material before re-testing formally.*

- [ ] **Step 6.** Work through the Sample Questions section in the official guide. Answer each one yourself first, scrolling carefully so you don't accidentally reveal the answer early, then read the explanation once you've committed to an answer.

---

## Phase 5: Second Official Pass
*Goal: re-baseline with full explanations this time.*

- [ ] **Step 7.** Take the official Anthropic practice exam again, but this time read the explanation after every single question. Track your results at the end.

---

## Phase 6: Targeted Gap-Closing
*Goal: mop up whatever is still shaky.*

- [ ] **Step 8.** Go back through the links and documents above (and google further) for whatever areas are still giving you trouble.

---

## Phase 7: Timed Practice
*Goal: test under real conditions.*

- [ ] **Step 9.** Take the Cyberskill test again, this time in timed mode. Track your results.

---

## Phase 8: Cold Test
*Goal: check generalization, not memorization.*

- [ ] **Step 10.** Take the [certificationpractice.com test](https://certificationpractice.com/practice-exams/anthropic-claude-certified-architect-foundations). It doesn't give you any analysis of your answers, which is fine — the point of this one isn't to learn from it, it's to check whether you can actually handle questions you haven't seen before, rather than just recognizing patterns from the tests you've already studied. Track your results.

---

## Phase 9: Decision Gate and Exam Day

- [ ] **Step 11.** At this point you should have a decent read on where you stand. If your latest scores are sitting around 70%, you're not quite there yet — go deeper on the theory for whichever domains you're struggling with most, and run through the tests in **Additional Practice Sources** section below. If you're at 90% or higher, you're probably ready. From here it's just a matter of picking your exam date and, *the day before, taking the official Anthropic practice exam one more time* — the actual exam might have some of the same questions, so this gives your answers (and your confidence) a small boost going in.
